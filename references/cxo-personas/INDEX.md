# CXO Persona Library — Index

## Overview

This document indexes 18 C-suite and senior executive personas organized across 6 categories. The library powers a **sales AI agent** that helps sales reps prepare for customer executive meetings — enabling rapid persona identification, priority alignment, and engagement coaching tailored to each executive's mindset.

---

## How to Use This Document

### For AI Agents and LLMs

When preparing a sales rep for a meeting, **confirm the target persona first** using the Title Mapping Guide or Topic Routing Guide below. Then load the relevant persona file and use these sections to align coaching:

- **Priorities** — What the executive cares about most right now
- **Pain Points** — Where they feel pressure and frustration
- **AI Opportunities** — Where your solution can create value
- **How They Talk & How to Talk to Them** — Language, tone, and framing guidance
- **How to Write for This Persona** — Appropriate vocabulary and style

### For Human Users

These personas provide the **audience intelligence layer** for sales preparation. Use them to understand what executives care about, what language resonates, and what to avoid saying. Pair with account research and deal context for maximum effectiveness.

## Cross-Persona Dynamics

### Common Executive Buying Committees

- **Enterprise AI deals:** CAIO (champion) + CIO (infrastructure) + CISO (security review) + CFO (budget) + General Counsel (compliance) + Head of Procurement (commercial terms)
- **Digital transformation:** CDxO (champion) + CIO (integration) + CFO (funding) + COO (operational readiness) + CHRO (change management)
- **Security purchases:** CISO (champion/decision maker) + CIO (infrastructure) + General Counsel (compliance) + CFO (budget) + Head of Procurement (negotiation)
- **Revenue/sales tech:** CRO or VP of Sales (champion) + CFO (ROI validation) + CIO (integration) + Head of Procurement (commercial terms)
- **Data platform deals:** CDO (champion) + CIO (infrastructure) + CAO (analytics requirements) + CISO (data security) + CFO (budget)

### Champion vs. Blocker vs. Decision Maker Patterns

| Role | Typical Position | Notes |
|------|-----------------|-------|
| CEO | Final decision maker | Engaged on strategic, $1M+ deals; delegates operational purchases |
| CFO | Economic buyer / blocker | Can kill any deal on ROI grounds; must be addressed early |
| CIO | Gatekeeper / blocker | Controls integration and infrastructure; bypass at your peril |
| CISO | Veto authority | Default posture is skepticism; security review can stall or kill deals |
| General Counsel | Gatekeeper / blocker | Rarely champions but can single-handedly block on risk or compliance |
| Head of Procurement | Process owner / negotiator | Controls commercial terms and timeline; engage early, not as an afterthought |
| Line-of-business leaders (CRO, CMO, COO, CHRO) | Champions | Bring the business need; need internal allies to navigate IT and finance |
| CAIO, CDO, CAO, CINO | Technical champions / influencers | Strong influence on technology selection; may lack direct budget authority |

### Multi-Threading Recommendations

- **Never single-thread.** Enterprise deals with one contact are fragile — one job change or priority shift kills the deal.
- **Map the buying committee early.** Identify champion, economic buyer, technical evaluator, and legal/procurement contacts within the first two meetings.
- **Engage procurement proactively.** Sellers who treat procurement as an obstacle face longer cycles and worse terms. Sellers who engage them as partners close faster.
- **Align messaging by persona.** The CEO cares about strategic impact; the CFO cares about ROI; the CIO cares about integration; the CISO cares about risk. Same solution, different story for each.
- **Watch for the "pocket veto."** CISO, General Counsel, and CIO can quietly block deals through extended reviews. Proactive engagement prevents surprises.

## Usage Notes

### For AI Agents

- **Always confirm the persona** before coaching. Use the Title Mapping Guide if only a job title is available.
- **Load the full persona file** for the target executive — the index provides summaries only.
- **Use "Priorities" and "Pain Points"** to frame the conversation around what the executive actually cares about.
- **Use "AI Opportunities"** to identify where your solution creates value in the executive's language.
- **Use "How They Talk & How to Talk to Them"** to calibrate tone, vocabulary, and framing.
- **Cross-reference the Topic Routing Guide** when multiple executives are involved in a deal.
- **For multi-persona deals**, load each relevant persona and tailor messaging per executive.

### For Human Users

- Pair these personas with **account-specific research** (10-K filings, earnings calls, LinkedIn profiles, news) for maximum relevance.
- Use the **Topic Routing Guide** to identify which executives to engage based on the solution you're positioning.
- Use **Cross-Persona Dynamics** to plan multi-threaded engagement strategies.
- These personas reflect general patterns — always validate against the specific individual and organization.
